Island BBQ Pulled Pork

 

A BBQ pulled pork sandwich topped with diced pineapple

 

I've been making pulled pork sandwiches for years and decided one day to try and change things up a bit by mixing some barbecue sauce with a teriyaki marinade and then topping the sandwich with pineapple. This twist on the tradition pulled pork sandwich gives it a sweet tangy flavor reminiscent on the island life.

 

Ingredients:

6 serving

 
  • 1 pork shoulder (roughly 12-15 pounds)) ,
  • 20 ounces of bbq (bottle or homemade if possible)
  • 20 ounces of teriyaki marinade(bottle or homemade if possible)
  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup coconut cream
  • 1 can sliced pineapple (or fresh cut slices from a pineapple)
  • 2 tbs fresh minced garlic
  • 2 tbs onion powder
  • 1/2 tbs fresh ground pepper
  • 6 buns (I prefer Brioche)

Directions:

  1. Trim excess fat from pork shoulder and then cut into four even pieces.
  2. Put the pieces of pork shoulder into a sealable contained and add all ingredients excluding the barbecue sauce and sliced pineapple. Let marinade for 3 hours to 24 hours.
  3. Empty pork shoulder and all contents of the container into the pressure cooker. Add the barbecue sauce and mix it in.
  4. Cook for 70 minutes in pressure cooker and the natural release for 15 minutes.
  5. Separate the meat from the sauce and shred until sandwich worthy (save the sauce!).
  6. Set shredded meat into a toasted bun, drizzle with sauce from cooking the pork and top with a slice of pineapple.
